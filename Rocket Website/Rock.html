Mars and Rockets

Other than Mars being right next to us in the solar system, there are many other reasons to visit the “Red Planet”. Humans have long wondered what happens on Mars; from the sci-fi geeks to the conspiracy theorists, they all believe that Mars is an important planet for humans to explore. First, humans want to search for life on Mars. Recently, many astrobiologists have hypothesized and confirmed that Mars once had liquid water flowing throughout, as well as nutrients used to supply life and a magnetic field similar to Earth's atmosphere which protects life (Anderson, 2017). Additionally, many geologists and scientists are bewildered about the planet’s evolution and its surface as it could lead scientists to answering the decades old question of whether there is any water or life on Mars. Lastly, Mars is seen as a very important stepping stone for future exploration of other planets and solar systems. Martian rovers like Sojourner, Opportunity, Spirit, Curiosity, and Perseverance have all gone to collect samples of rock and soil, revealing key details about its formation and evolution. A few important instruments on board these rovers are MOXIE, to produce oxygen from the Mar’s atmosphere, RIMFAX, to analyze the geological structure of the planet’s subsurface, and SHERLOC, to detect organic compounds (Instruments-NASA Mars, 2020). Reaching Mars requires a rocket, which in turn requires a propulsion system for transporting humans and resources from the surface of Earth to Mars. A propulsion system can either be an air-breathing or non-air-breathing system. An air-breathing system is an engine that requires atmospheric oxygen to allow for combustion. A non-air breathing system carries its own supply of oxygen. Rockets that leave the earth’s atmosphere use a non-air breathing system because in the vacuum of space, there is no oxygen. A rocket has four forces acting on it during its flight: gravity, thrust, drag, and lift. When discussing rocket propulsion systems, gravity, thrust, and drag are the most important to analyze in order to create the optimal design for the rocket based on its mission.
